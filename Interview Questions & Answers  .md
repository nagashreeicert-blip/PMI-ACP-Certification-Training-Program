## 1. What is Agile?
**Answer:**  
Agile is an iterative and incremental project delivery approach focused on continuous value delivery, customer collaboration, responding to change, and empowering self-organizing teams. Agile replaces heavy documentation with rapid feedback loops and adaptive planning.

---

## 2. What is the PMI-ACP Certification?
**Answer:**  
The PMI-ACP (Agile Certified Practitioner) certification validates hands-on experience with **Scrum, Kanban, XP, Lean, and hybrid Agile practices**. It demonstrates your ability to apply Agile principles in real-world project environments.

---

## 3. What are the main Agile Values?
**Answer (from Agile Manifesto):**
- Individuals & interactions over processes & tools  
- Working software over comprehensive documentation  
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation  
- Responding to change over following a plan  

---

## 4. What are Agile Principles?  
**Answer:**  
There are **12 Agile Principles**, including continuous delivery, welcome changing requirements, frequent delivery, close collaboration, motivated teams, face-to-face communication, sustainable pace, technical excellence, simplicity, self-organizing teams, and regular reflection/optimization.

---

## 5. What is a User Story?
**Answer:**  
A User Story represents a small deliverable piece of value.  
**Format:**  
- *As a* ___  
- *I want* ___  
- *So that* ___  

---

## 6. What is the Definition of Done (DoD)?
**Answer:**  
DoD is a shared agreement on completion criteria. It ensures transparency, quality, and prevents technical debt.

---

## 7. What is Velocity in Agile?
**Answer:**  
Velocity is the measure of the amount of work (story points) completed in a sprint.  
Used for forecasting release timelines.

---

## 8. What is Sprint Planning?
**Answer:**  
A meeting where the team defines:
- Sprint Goal  
- Stories to commit  
- Tasks required to complete them  

---

## 9. What is a Burndown Chart?
**Answer:**  
A visual chart showing remaining work vs. time. Helps track progress and predict completion.

---

## 10. What is Kanban?
**Answer:**  
A workflow visualization method focused on:
- Limiting Work-In-Progress (WIP)  
- Continuous delivery  
- Reducing bottlenecks  

---

## 11. What is Scrum?
**Answer:**  
An Agile framework with defined ceremonies:  
- Sprint Planning  
- Daily Scrum  
- Sprint Review  
- Sprint Retrospective  
And roles:  
- Product Owner  
- Scrum Master  
- Developers

---

## 12. What is the role of a Scrum Master?
**Answer:**  
A servant leader who:
- Removes impediments  
- Coaches the team  
- Facilitates ceremonies  
- Protects the team from external interference  

---

## 13. What is the role of a Product Owner?
**Answer:**  
Owns the Product Backlog, sets priorities, communicates vision, and ensures value delivery.

---

## 14. What is Technical Debt?
**Answer:**  
The cost of rework from choosing speed over quality.  
Examples: bad code, no tests, poor design.

---

## 15. What is Empirical Process Control?
**Answer:**  
Agile is based on:
- **Transparency**  
- **Inspection**  
- **Adaptation**  

---

## 16. What are Agile Estimation Techniques?
Common techniques:
- Planning Poker  
- T-shirt sizing  
- Fibonacci story points  
- Affinity estimation  

---

## 17. What is a Spike?
**Answer:**  
A time-boxed research activity to investigate unknowns.

---

## 18. What is a Retrospective?
**Answer:**  
A meeting to reflect on what went well, improvements, and action items for the next sprint.

---

## 19. What is the Agile Triangle?
**Answer:**  
Balances:
- Value  
- Quality  
- Constraints (time, cost, scope)

---

## 20. Why is PMI-ACP Important?
**Answer:**  
Because it proves:
- Multi-framework Agile expertise  
- Real-world Agile implementation experience  
- Capability to lead adaptive teams  
- Improved hiring potential for Scrum Master, Product Owner, and Agile Coach roles  

---

## 21. Difference Between Agile & Waterfall?
**Agile:** iterative, adaptive, fast feedback.  
**Waterfall:** sequential, plan-driven, rigid.

---

## 22. What are Agile Anti-Patterns?
Examples:
- Fake sprints  
- No retrospectives  
- Overcommitting  
- Ignoring Definition of Done  

---

## 23. What is MVP?
**Answer:**  
Minimum Viable Product ‚Äì smallest usable version delivering value.

---

## 24. What is Timeboxing?
**Answer:**  
Fixing maximum time for an activity (sprints, meetings, spikes).

---

## 25. What is Servant Leadership?
**Answer:**  
Leadership style where the leader focuses on team growth, empowerment, and removing obstacles rather than command-control.

---

### **Q: A stakeholder wants to add last-minute changes mid-sprint. What do you do?**
**Answer:**  
Explain sprint commitment and negotiate with the Product Owner. Add change to the backlog and consider it for the next sprint unless critical.

---

### **Q: Your team consistently misses sprint commitments. What is your action?**
**Answer:**  
- Analyze root causes  
- Improve estimation  
- Rebalance workload  
- Remove blockers  
- Re-establish sustainable pace  

---

### **Q: Your team is new to Agile. How do you help them adopt it?**
**Answer:**  
- Training  
- Coaching  
- Regular retrospectives  
- Start small with simple frameworks  
- Encourage transparency & collaboration
